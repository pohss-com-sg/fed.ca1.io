tips!

  1. Tapping into any MRT station before 7.45 am on weekdays (excluding public holidays) nets you up to a 50-cent discount on your rail fare.
  2. You can chain up to five transfers within a single journey to avoid paying multiple base fares, provided you follow these rules:
    • Maximum 45 minutes per transfer.
    • Maximum 120 minutes total travel duration for the whole trip.
    • You can exit one MRT station and enter a different MRT station within 15 minutes without breaking the continuous journey.
  3. Use Citymapper for the most accurate multi-modal route planning
  4. Use your commute for low-intensity cognitive tasks like reviewing flashcards, reading a book or listening to music.

get involved!

potential challenges

  1. Singapore's unpredictable rain or shine makes walking even short distances difficult.
    • Keep a compact umbrella handy at all times and map out sheltered linkways between destinations.
  2. Dealing with heavy overcrowding in peak-hour trains and long waiting times for buses could be stressful.
    • Use transit tracking apps to time departures perfectly and avoid standing needlessly on hot platforms.
  3. There could be friction from friends or family who prefer taking a private hire vehicle instead.
    • Take the lead on trip planning and gently advocate for the cost and efficiency benefits of taking public transport.
